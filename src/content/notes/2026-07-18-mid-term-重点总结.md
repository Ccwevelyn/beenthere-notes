<u>When Usability Testing Is Better</u>？

+ **Need real user feedback** on task completion, confusion points, or emotional reactions.
+ **Testing a live or high-fidelity prototype** and need to validate assumptions.
+ **Targeting specific user groups** (e.g., elderly, children, professionals) whose behavior may differ from expert expectations.

<u>When Expert Reviews Are Better</u>？

+ **In early design stages** and want fast, low-cost feedback.
+ **Need to catch heuristic violations** like inconsistent layouts
+ **Under time or budget constraints** and can't recruit users.



4. 顺序的重要性**（ER->UT)**

| **Efficiency** | **Expert Review** helps** eliminate obvious design flaws** before testing,** saving time and cost**. |
| --- | --- |
| **Focus** | It allows usability testing to **concentrate on deeper behavioral issues**, not surface-level mistakes. |
| **User Experience** | **Prevents** users** from getting stuck** on basic problems, improving the quality of feedback. |
| **Validation Loop** | **Expert** **assumptions** can** be validated through user behavio**r, creating a structured evaluation cycle. |


**Expert Review first** ensures the interface is clean enough for meaningful testing.

**Usability Testing second** provides <u>real-world validation</u> and behavioral insights. 

**<font style="color:#2F4BDA;">Reversing the order risks wasting resources and collecting low-quality data.  </font>**

**<font style="color:#2F4BDA;"></font>**

**<u>Risks of Reversing the Order</u>**

+ Testing sessions may focus on trivial problems. 测试过程可能集中在琐碎问题上，无法深入挖掘真正的可用性障碍  
+ You may** spend more time** fixing things that experts could've flagged earlier.



5. 3~5 EXPERT 原因

**Using 3–5 experts ensures a rich, reliable, and cost-effective evaluation.** It balances depth of insight with efficiency, making it the optimal range for most UX review scenarios.

#### 四、Expert review（<font style="color:rgb(38,38,38);">Think aloud protocol）</font>
1. Difference
2. Meaning

**Think-aloud protocol** is a technique used in usability testing and cognitive research where users are asked to _**say out loud what they are thinking**_ as they perform tasks.  

The** concurrent think-aloud protocol **is a usability testing method where participants **<u><font style="color:#DF2A3F;">verbally narrate</font></u>** their thoughts, feelings, and actions **while performing a task**

** Retrospective Think-Aloud (RTA) **is a usability testing method where participants **complete** a task and then, after the session, verbally **explain** their thoughts, feelings, and reasoning while reviewing a recording of their own performance

|  | **Concurrent Think-Aloud** | **Retrospective Think-Aloud** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Timing | **During** task | **After** task |
| Data freshness | Real-time | Memory-based |
| Task interference | Possible | None |
| Common use | Early-stage usability testing | Post-task analysis or interviews |




3. **Limitation**

<u> Concurrent Think-Aloud (CTA) </u> 

 用户在执行任务的同时 verbalize（说出）自己的想法  

| **Cognitive overload** | Speaking while performing tasks **increases mental effort**, which may affect task performance. | 同时操作和表达思维会增加认知负荷，可能影响任务表现。 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Behavioral distortion** | Verbalizing thoughts may** alter natural decision-making**, leading to less authentic behavior. | 说话可能改变用户的自然决策路径，导致行为不真实。 |
| **Surface-level insights** | Users may only express immediate thoughts, **lacking deeper reflection** or emotional context. | 用户可能只表达表层想法，缺乏深度反思或情绪背景。 |




Practical **Limitations** of CTA - **对环境和资源的要求**  

<!-- 这是一张图片，ocr 内容为： -->
![](https://cdn.nlark.com/yuque/0/2025/png/42556572/1763178946450-5c2a22a0-fe04-4ff9-8622-4b3833db5bd9.png)

| Requires controlled environment | CTA often needs a** quiet, distraction-free** space to ensure users can speak and perform tasks naturally. | 通常需要安静、无干扰的环境，<br/>确保用户能自然地操作并 verbalize。 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Needs one-way mirror or observation setup | For professional-grade CTA, usability labs may use**<font style="color:#DF2A3F;"> one-way mirrors </font>**or **live observation rooms**, which are **<font style="color:#2F4BDA;">costly</font>**. | 专业测试常用单面镜或观察室，成本高昂，小公司难以负担。 |
| Demands skilled facilitation | Facilitators must guide without interfering, which requires training and experience. | 引导者需具备专业技巧，避免干扰用户行为，培训成本高。 |
| Not scalable for remote or agile teams | In fast-paced or remote settings, setting up CTA sessions may be impractical. | 对于远程或敏捷团队，组织 CTA 测试难度大，不易扩展。 |




 <u>Retrospective Think-Aloud (RTA)  </u>

 用户在完成任务后 verbalize（回顾）自己的思维过程，通常配合视频回放  

| Memory bias | Users may** forget or misrepresent **their actual thoughts and motivations. | 用户可能忘记或误解当时的真实想法与动机。 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Rationalization | Users tend to** reconstruct their behavior logically**, which may **not reflect** real-time decisions. | 用户倾向于“合理化”行为，而非真实还原当时的决策。 |


---

 Why **Concurrent Think-Aloud** Is Often **Better**？  

| **Captures real-time cognition** | CTA records users' thoughts as they happen, providing direct access to their decision-making process. |
| --- | --- |
| **Reveals immediate confusion or hesitation** | You can observe where users struggle, hesitate, or express uncertainty in the moment, which is often lost in retrospective recall. |
| **Minimizes memory distortion** | Since users verbalize during the task, there's no reliance on memory, reducing the risk of rationalization or forgetting. |
| **More authentic reactions** | Emotional responses (e.g., frustration, surprise) are captured live, offering richer usability insights. |
| **Supports task-context linkage** | Observers can directly correlate user comments with specific interface elements or actions, improving diagnostic accuracy. |
